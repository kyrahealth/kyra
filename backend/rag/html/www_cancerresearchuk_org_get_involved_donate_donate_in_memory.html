    Donate in memory

    Giving in memory of a loved one is a special way to honour their life.

    Donate in a loved one's memory

    When you make a donation in memory to Cancer Research UK, you're making a donation in honour of your loved one that will help transform the lives of future generations.

    Donate in a loved one's name

    If you are paying in a gift in will on behalf of a loved one, then please fill out our Gift in Will form.

    Other ways to donate in memory

    Organise a funeral or memorial collection

    A funeral or memorial collection can be a meaningful way for a group of people to honour a loved one.

    When your collection is complete you can pay in the money that has been raised using the online form below.

    Pay in your collection

    Set up a Cancer Research UK Tribute Page

    A tribute page is an online memorial space that you can create and personalise in honour of a loved one.

    It’s a place for you, family and friends to remember and celebrate the life of someone special to you.

    Create a tribute page

    Find an existing tribute page

    Where your donation goes

    Your donation will help us prevent, detect and treat over 200 types of cancer. Here is just a snapshot of some of the work our researchers are doing right now. 

    Preventing Cancer

    We're working on LungVax - the world's first vaccine to prevent lung cancer in people with a high risk of the disease. If successful, this vaccine has the potential to save tens of thousands of lives in the UK alone

    Detecting Cancer

    Our researchers are hoping to develop a risk assessment for breast cancer that all women can have when they reach 30. The assessment could help women identified as high risk get access to early screening, which aims to detect cancer at an early stage when treatment is more likely to be successful.

    Treating Cancer

    We're working to develop innovative new drug technologies for children's and young people's cancers. As well as helping more young cancer patients survive cancer, these targeted drugs could also cause far fewer side effects than other treatments.

    We’re here for you

    Grief after losing a loved one can feel overwhelming and we want to be here for you at this difficult time. If you need support, we have a number of available resources:

    • Call our nurse helpline on 0808 800 4040 (Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm)

    • Visit the Cancer Chat support forum

    • Get information on coping with grief

    You can also speak to our friendly team if you have any questions about donating in memory or setting up a Tribute Page:

    • Phone: 0300 123 1022 (Monday to Friday, 8am - 8pm, Saturday & Sunday, 9am - 5pm)

    • Email: supporter.services@cancer.org.uk
